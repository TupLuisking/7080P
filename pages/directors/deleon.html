DE LEON

1947

Vision and Versatility

Never afraid to follow the beat of his own drum. MDL: jack of all trades, master of all.

Navigate with ↑ & ↓ arrow keys

Family of filmmakers: MDL’s father Manuel De Leon was the film producer of films Anak Dalita (1956) and Badjao (1957) and his grandmother Narcisa De León was the president of LVN Pictures

His interest in filmmaking began when he took his master’s at Heidelberg University in Germany

Used film mainly as a source of activism against oppression and injustice in the PH (strongly opposed to the Marcos and Duterte regime)

A stickler for personal artistic integrity, MDL departed from filmmaking in the late 90s because he found no interest in the commercial films being made and also due to the rise of piracy

He has an extremely varied style, ranging from horror (Itim, 1976) to romance (Kung Mangarap Ka’t Magising, 1977) all the way to satirical comedy (Kakabakaba Ka Ba?, 1980) but consistently tackles social issues and cultural topics at a deep level regardless of genre

Later even went further with his versatility by directing the experimental-narrative film Bayaning Third World in 1999

Innovated and enriched local cinema through the introduction of filmmaking techniques such as computer graphics animation, digital filmmaking, and historical deconstruction

"I come from a generation of moviemakers where a film, or better yet a movie, is just that, a movie, plain and simple, a visual way of telling stories that hopefully will find an audience."
